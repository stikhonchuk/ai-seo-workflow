## SEO Writing Best Practices

### 1. Title Tag Optimization
**Format:** Primary Keyword | Secondary Keyword | Brand Name
**Length:** 50-60 characters
**Examples:**
- Best Running Shoes for Women 2026 | Expert Reviews | [YOUR-DOMAIN]
- How to Clean Leather Shoes | Complete Care Guide | [YOUR-DOMAIN]
- Women's Sneakers | Shop Latest Styles | [YOUR-DOMAIN]

### 2. Meta Description Optimization
**Length:** 150-160 characters
**Include:** Primary keyword, benefit, call-to-action
**Examples:**
- "Discover the best running shoes for women in 2026. Expert reviews, buying guide, and recommendations for every foot type. Shop now at [YOUR-DOMAIN]!"
- "Learn how to clean leather shoes properly with our step-by-step guide. Keep your footwear looking new for years. Professional tips inside!"

### 3. URL Structure
**Best Practices:**
- Keep URLs short and descriptive
- Use hyphens to separate words
- Include primary keyword
- Avoid unnecessary words (the, and, or, etc.)
- Use lowercase only

**Good Examples:**
- /blog/best-running-shoes-women
- /how-to-clean-leather-shoes
- /women/sneakers
- /mens-work-boots

**Bad Examples:**
- /blog/post-12345
- /category/products/women/shoes/sneakers/casual
- /The-Best-Running-Shoes-For-Women-In-2026

### 4. Heading Structure (H1-H6)

**Rules:**
- Only ONE H1 per page (page title)
- Use H2 for main sections
- Use H3 for subsections under H2
- Include keywords naturally in headings
- Make headings descriptive and clear

**Example Structure:**
```
H1: Best Running Shoes for Women in 2026
  H2: How to Choose Running Shoes
    H3: Consider Your Foot Type
    H3: Determine Your Running Style
    H3: Set Your Budget
  H2: Top 10 Running Shoes Reviewed
    H3: 1. Nike Air Zoom Pegasus - Best Overall
    H3: 2. Brooks Ghost - Best for Beginners
  H2: Frequently Asked Questions
    H3: What are the best running shoes for flat feet?
    H3: How often should I replace running shoes?
```

### 5. Keyword Usage

**Keyword Density:** 1-2% (natural placement, not forced)

**Where to Include Primary Keyword:**
- Title tag (H1)
- First paragraph (within first 100 words)
- At least one H2 heading
- URL
- Meta description
- Alt text (at least one image)
- Conclusion paragraph

**Where to Include Secondary Keywords:**
- H2 and H3 headings
- Throughout body content
- Image alt text
- Internal link anchor text

**Keyword Variations:**
Use natural variations and synonyms:
- Primary: "women's running shoes"
- Variations: "running shoes for women," "ladies running sneakers," "women's athletic footwear"

### 6. Content Length Guidelines

**Blog Articles:**
- Short guides: 800-1,200 words
- Standard articles: 1,500-2,000 words
- Comprehensive guides: 2,500-3,500 words
- Pillar content: 3,500+ words

**Product Pages:**
- Minimum: 300 words
- Recommended: 500-800 words
- Premium products: 1,000+ words

**Category Pages:**
- Minimum: 200 words
- Recommended: 400-600 words

### 7. Internal Linking Strategy

**Guidelines:**
- Include 3-5 internal links per article
- Link to related articles and product pages
- Use descriptive anchor text (not "click here")
- Link to pillar content from supporting articles
- Link from high-authority pages to newer content

**Anchor Text Examples:**
- Good: "best running shoes for flat feet"
- Good: "our complete shoe care guide"
- Bad: "click here"
- Bad: "read more"

### 8. External Linking

**Best Practices:**
- Link to 1-2 authoritative sources per article
- Link to research, studies, or expert sources
- Use "nofollow" for untrusted sources
- Open external links in new tab
- Choose highly authoritative domains (.edu, .gov, major publications)

**Good External Link Sources:**
- Medical journals (for health-related content)
- Industry publications
- Brand official websites
- Research institutions
- Government health organizations

### 9. Image Optimization

#### Hero Image Requirements (MANDATORY)

Every blog article MUST have a unique hero image before publication.

**Specifications:**
- **Aspect ratio:** 16:9 (1920x1080px recommended)
- **Style:** Professional, editorial, lifestyle photography feel
- **Color palette:** Warm, inviting, premium feel
- **No text on images** - Text overlays added in CMS
- **No people's faces** - Avoid licensing complications
- **No brand logos** - Keep images brand-neutral

**AI Image Generation:**
- **Recommended tools:** Midjourney, DALL-E 3, Stable Diffusion, Leonardo.ai
- **Consistency:** Use same AI tool for all images in a content batch
- **Prompts:** Include specific hero image prompts in content calendar
- **Save seed numbers** (if tool supports) for consistent style

**Hero Image Prompt Template:**
```
[Scene description], [product/shoes description], [surface/setting], [lighting description], [photography style], [color palette], [mood/atmosphere]. No people, no text. 16:9 aspect ratio.
```

**Example Prompt:**

#### General Image Optimization

**File Names:**
- Use descriptive names with keywords
- Use hyphens to separate words
- Good: womens-running-shoes-nike-pegasus.jpg
- Bad: IMG_12345.jpg

**Alt Text:**
- Describe the image clearly
- Include relevant keywords naturally
- Good: "Woman wearing Nike Air Zoom Pegasus running shoes on track"
- Bad: "shoes"

**File Size:**
- Compress images to under 100KB when possible
- Use WebP format for better compression
- Lazy load images below the fold

**Dimensions:**
- Featured images: 1200 x 630px (for social sharing)
- Product images: 800 x 800px minimum
- In-content images: 600-800px width

### 10. Call-to-Action (CTA) Best Practices

**Placement:**
- Within first 2 paragraphs (soft CTA)
- Middle of content (secondary CTA)
- End of content (primary CTA)

**CTA Examples:**
- "Shop Our Collection of [Product]"
- "Find Your Perfect [Product]"
- "Browse [Number] More [Product] Options"
- "Get [X]% Off Your First Order"

**CTA Button Text:**
- Keep it short (2-4 words)
- Use action words
- Create urgency when appropriate
- Examples: "Shop Now," "View Collection," "Learn More," "Get Started"

## Writing Style Guide

### Tone & Voice
- **Professional but approachable:** Expert knowledge without jargon
- **Helpful and informative:** Focus on solving problems
- **Confident but not pushy:** Make recommendations without hard selling
- **Clear and concise:** Get to the point quickly

### Writing Tips

**Do:**
- Write in second person ("you") to connect with readers
- Use short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)
- Include bullet points and lists for scannability
- Break up text with subheadings every 300 words
- Use active voice ("Choose shoes that fit" vs "Shoes that fit should be chosen")
- Include specific examples and recommendations
- Back up claims with data or sources

**Don't:**
- Use jargon or technical terms without explanation
- Write long, rambling paragraphs
- Stuff keywords unnaturally
- Make claims you can't support
- Copy content from other websites
- Use clickbait or misleading titles
- Include irrelevant information

### Russian Market Considerations

Since [YOUR-DOMAIN] targets Russian speakers:

**Language:**
- Write content in Russian for main market
- Consider bilingual content for international products
- Use proper Russian SEO practices (Yandex optimization)

**Cultural Considerations:**
- Reference Russian sizing standards
- Include metric measurements
- Consider Russian shopping holidays and seasons
- Reference local brands and preferences

**Yandex-Specific SEO:**
- Yandex values content freshness highly
- Include precise geo-targeting if serving specific regions
- Use Cyrillic URLs when possible
- Register with Yandex Webmaster Tools

## Content Creation Workflow

Follow this workflow for every piece of content:

### Phase 1: Planning
1. Choose content piece from [publication calendar](../content/calendars/)
2. Review the content brief (keywords, intent, word count)
3. Select appropriate template from this guide
4. Create AI image prompts (hero + supporting images)

### Phase 2: Writing
5. Write first draft focusing on value and clarity
6. Include all required elements (CTAs, links, schema)
7. Save draft to `content/drafts/` directory

### Phase 3: Review (MANDATORY)
8. **Run `/review-article`** to get multi-critic evaluation
   - This runs 7 specialized critics automatically
   - Generates detailed review with scores and fixes

9. Review the critic report:
   - **Score â‰¥8**: Ready for final review
   - **Score 6-7**: Address major issues, re-run review
   - **Score <6**: Significant revision needed

10. Fix issues identified by critics (priority order):
    - Critical issues first
    - Major issues second
    - Minor issues if time allows

11. Re-run `/review-article` after fixes to verify improvement

### Phase 4: Publication
12. Generate images using AI prompts
13. Final human review (user checks Russian quality)
14. Upload to CMS with all metadata
15. Submit to Yandex Webmaster for indexing

### Phase 5: Monitoring
16. Track initial rankings (Week 1)
17. Monitor Search Console/Webmaster (Week 2-4)
18. Update content if needed based on performance

## Critic Review System

### Available Critics

Run `/review-article` to automatically evaluate content across 7 dimensions:

| Critic | Model | What It Checks |
|--------|-------|----------------|
| **SEO** | Sonnet | Keywords, meta tags, structure, links, schema |
| **Russian Language** | Opus | Grammar, natural phrasing, register, calques |
| **E-E-A-T** | Sonnet | Experience, expertise, authority, trust signals |
| **User Intent** | Sonnet | Search intent satisfaction, completeness |
| **Readability** | Haiku | Sentence/paragraph structure, scannability |
| **Commercial** | Haiku | CTAs, product links, conversion path |
| **Image Prompts** | Haiku | AI prompt validation, brand consistency |

### Review Commands

```bash
# Full review (all 7 critics)
/review-article

# Quick review (summary scores only - faster/cheaper)
/review-article --quick

# Single critic (for targeted fixes)
/review-article --critic=seo
/review-article --critic=russian
/review-article --critic=eeat
/review-article --critic=intent
/review-article --critic=readability
/review-article --critic=commercial
/review-article --critic=images
```

### Score Interpretation

| Overall Score | Status | Action |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| 9-10 | Excellent | Ready to publish |
| 8 | Good | Minor polish, then publish |
| 6-7 | Needs Work | Fix issues, re-review |
| 4-5 | Major Issues | Significant revision needed |
| <4 | Rewrite | Start over with new approach |

### Review Output Location

Reviews are saved to: `content/reviews/review-[filename]-[date].md`

Use these reports to:
- Track content quality over time
- Identify patterns in issues
- Improve writing process
